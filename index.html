The stakes

Why compliance is not optional

The DPDP Act creates legally enforceable obligations for every clinic that handles digital patient data — with significant financial penalties for non-compliance.

₹250 Cr
Failure of data securityMaximum penalty for failing to implement reasonable safeguards to protect patient data.
₹200 Cr
Breach notification failureFailing to notify patients and the Data Protection Board within 72 hours of a breach.
₹150 Cr
Violation of patient rightsFailing to honour patients' rights of access, correction, erasure, or grievance redressal.
₹50 Cr
Consent & notice failuresCollecting patient data without proper informed consent or a compliant consent notice.

Every clinic that stores patient records digitally is a Data Fiduciary under the Act.

That includes registration forms, prescriptions, lab reports, billing records — and even the phone numbers in your front desk's contact list. If any of this is in digital form, the DPDP Act applies to your clinic.

The good news: compliance is achievable. With the right documentation, staff training, and processes in place, a small clinic can be fully compliant well ahead of the deadline — without hiring a full-time legal or compliance team.

  • Patient consent must be obtained before any data is collected
  • Patients have six legally enforceable rights over their own data
  • Data breaches must be reported within 72 hours
  • All staff who handle patient data must be trained on their obligations
  • Third-party vendors must have formal data processing agreements in place

VM Digital Suraksha was founded with a single purpose: to make India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act accessible and achievable for small, independent clinics that lack the resources of large hospital groups.

The DPDP Act is the most significant data protection legislation India has seen — and it applies equally to every practice, regardless of size. A clinic with three staff and fifty patients has the same legal obligations as a multi-speciality hospital. Yet the guidance, tools, and support available have almost entirely been designed for large enterprises.

We work exclusively with small clinics — which means our processes, documentation, and training are built around how a small practice actually operates. We speak plain language, not legalese. We work around your schedule. And we leave you with everything you need to stay compliant, not just to pass an audit.
